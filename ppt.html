I enjoy creating engaging presentations that capture the audience's attention. I believe a great visual is half the effort needed to make a presentation for any situation a success. My current skills are particular to Microsoft Powerpoint, and I enjoy employing the software's morph transitions and 3D imports to design and build these presentations.

Gender Shades

The presentation was created for my class, Infosci 103 - Computation, Society, and Culture. The topic was based on the research paper, "Gender Shades" by MIT researcher, Joy Buolamwini and Microsoft researcher, Timnit Gebru. I utilized morph transitions to transition the study's information to distribute it in a clear, understandable way. I also utilized Microsoft's slide zoom in feature to divide the research section into three clear visual sections: dataset, commercial gender classification, and evaluation. The color scheme uses grey, white, black, and teal hues to depict a clean but modern aesthetic to fit the topic of new technology.

西雅图旅行

In spring 2023, for my intro level Chinese class, I created a presentation on traveling to Seattle for the class final. The presentation makes use of the time of the year with a spring theme. The 3D models, imported from Sketchfab include sakura leaves and Seattle landmarks and specialties. The 3D models are also the main animations to draw attention to the places and things that can make Seattle more appealing to travelers. The colors used are pale green, white, and a but of baby blue to fit the spring theme.
